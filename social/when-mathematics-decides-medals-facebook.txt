At the Glasgow 2026 Commonwealth Games, para-powerlifting medals will be decided using more than the weight on the bar.

Because the usual bodyweight categories have been combined into lightweight and heavyweight divisions, athletes of very different sizes will be compared using a mathematical scoring system.

These algorithms are intended to make competition fair, but no formula is completely neutral. Depending on how bodyweight is accounted for, lighter or heavier athletes may be affected differently.

I have written a new article explaining how these scoring systems work, why they matter in Glasgow, and what this tells us about the wider use of algorithms in sport.
