At the Glasgow 2026 Commonwealth Games, the athlete who lifts the most weight will not necessarily win gold.

Para-powerlifting will use combined lightweight and heavyweight divisions, meaning performances across different bodyweights must be compared using a mathematical scoring system.

These algorithms are designed to make competition fair. But research across powerlifting and para-powerlifting shows that no scoring system is completely neutral across the full bodyweight spectrum.

That matters because, in Glasgow, the algorithm will not simply supplement the competition format. It will help determine who stands on the podium.

I have written a new article exploring why weight classes exist, how bodyweight-adjusted scoring works, and what para-powerlifting can teach us about the growing role of algorithms in sport.

It is not an argument against the competition format. It is a reminder that fairness should be measured, tested and explained—particularly when mathematics is helping decide medals.
